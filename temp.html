Software Engineering Manager @ Sky

May 2015 - current

I was originally brought into the department I joined Sky with, to help establish a good culture and develop good engineering practices through Agile ways of working. This was done by leading by example and embedding some of the good engineering practices I learned at OpenBet, taking line management and career progression of employees seriously and changing the mind-set of the department to be focussed on building the software for the long term through ownership and by placing a priority in non-functional requirements like supportability, performance etc. and not just short term project deadlines.

Achievements / Contributions

Helped introduce a new culture and more agile way of working into one of the departments, through a long-term transformation initiative. The department had a more waterfall project driven approach before that. This was followed up by a continuous improvements workgroup.
Took over a project midway through with a team of 40+ people working on it and helped get it delivered successful while also getting the team into a mind-set of building the software for the long term through ownership and supportability rather than just meeting project driven deadlines without consideration of non-functional requirements like they were doing previously under the leadership of a Project Manager with no engineering background.
Line managed as well as coached other managers in the department as well as individuals within my own team
Re-motivated a Gonzales team on taking it over to be productive and happy while delivering important projects and functionality to 4 different production systems including launches in Italy, Germany and Spain.
New team created from scratch that successfully delivered new MAP (Master Acquisition Portal) functionality for the CSC (Content Supply Chain) project.
Organised department hack days and team socials
Sky Agile community - attended Agile community meet ups and brown bags
Contributed to the growth of the departments and engineers at Sky by carrying out recruitment - filtering of CVs, telephone and face 2 face interviews for Senior Developers, Analysts and Development Manager
Managed team budgets and finances
Contributed to the creation and introduction of a new more formal career progression path for developers across all of Sky through the job families work group by:
  1. Creating a new Software Engineer's Career Matrix at Sky
  2. Attending promotion panels for developers in other departments to adhere to the new matrix for promotion evaluation

Software Engineering Manager @ OpenBet

March 2003 - May 2015

Achievements / Contributions

As Technical Lead, I led a successful delivery and launch of a new Sports Betting website, back-end trading software and retail integration for a new OpenBet Customer in Canada, Atlantic Lottery, all while working with the client from their offices in New Brunswick. I was involved in the project from the start and responsible for the entire project lifecycle from requirements gathering, solution design, development, testing, training and delivery to production.
As Development Manager, I led a large team and was technically responsible for the Coral Account of up to 40 people distributed in 3 teams across London, Romania and Athens. As well as delivering new functionality through the year a part of a regular release cycle, Coral had a very successful Grand National from a system performance point of view with their highest peak bets/minute achieved to that point.
At OpenBet I have been involved in and have experience in all stages of the software development life cycle - RFP responses, requirements gathering, analysis, solution design, development, testing, releases, production support, performance monitoring and improvements. Engineers are involved in all these activities and not just restricted to development and engineering managers are responsible for all of these phases for their team/client account.
On the Coral account I helped transition the team from a waterfall methodology to a more Agile way of working by introducing Scrum, having a Coral Product Owner embedded in the team, introducing more regular releases and helped the team become more efficient by having less context switching by splitting the team into distinct functions to allow team to concentrate on priorities from the relevant business owner for that particular business stream – BAU & Production Support, Sprint Feature Development, Analysis, Releases
I developed and built up working relationships with all the clients whose teams I worked on. This was particularly important as a Technical Lead/Development manager where as the technical point of contact for OpenBet for the account there was lots of stakeholder management with senior stakeholders from the client like CTOs, Sportsbook Directors, Senior Project Managers, Steering Boards etc. This was done by being honest, creating good communication lines and building trust with a proven track record of delivering for them over time.
While line managing people in lots of different engineering roles (Developers, Analysts, Testers, Technical Leads, Scrum Masters) through coaching and mentoring, I have helped with their career progression to roles such as Senior Developer, Technical Lead and Development Manager.
Through roles such as Senior Developer, Technical Lead and Development Manager I had technical responsibility for the accounts, which meant taking responsibility for the quality of software being produced, assisting development staff in developing solutions to software-related problems, execution of regular code reviews to ensure the production system was performant, resilient, PCI compliant and scalable to meet demand. This involved collaborating and working closely with client teams and 3rd parties (PMs, Architects, Analysts, Test Leads, Release Managers) in arriving at and building solutions to meet their needs and requirements while still adhering to OpenBet's high technical standards.
Collaborated with other OpenBet Technical leads, Development managers, the CTO and Commercial Account Directors in applying OpenBet company strategy and objectives (commercially and technically) to the team and client account I was managing. From authoring and policing technical standards, promoting code reuse, exchanging ideas and to planning the roll out of new strategic OpenBet products for the commercial success of the account.
Worked on the OpenBet support team for 6 months, supporting all clients live production systems as the first line of support including out of hours. This high pressure environment was a great learning experience with exposure to all the client code bases and different type of client accounts (small and large). It helped focus on what needs doing when building software solutions from a non-functional point of view for resiliency and performance.
As a full stack Senior Developer I was involved multiple website redesigns. I was lead developer for the BlueSq Sportsbook redesign ahead of the 2006 world cup. I was Technical Lead for Sportech for their football pools website redesign and on Totesport I was Senior Developer on the team that delivered the new Tote website, all high profile project launches for the company and the clients at the time.
